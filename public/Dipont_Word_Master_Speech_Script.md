## Slide 2: Market Pain Points (2-3 minutes)

Before I show you our solution, let me paint a picture of the challenges we're addressing.

### Pain Point #1: Boring Learning Experience

Traditional vocabulary learning methods rely heavily on rote memorization — endless flashcards, repetitive drills, and monotonous exercises. Research shows that the average student abandons vocabulary apps within just **two weeks**. Why? Because without engagement, motivation dies.

### Pain Point #2: Difficult Progress Tracking

Parents and teachers struggle to monitor student progress in real-time. There's no easy way to see who's falling behind, which words are causing trouble, or whether the study time is actually productive. This makes targeted intervention nearly impossible.

### Pain Point #3: Isolated Learning

Learning vocabulary in isolation is lonely and monotonous. Students miss out on the peer interaction and healthy competition that naturally motivates them in other activities. Without a social element, forming consistent study habits becomes an uphill battle.

These three problems — boring content, invisible progress, and isolated practice — are exactly what Dipont Word Master was built to solve.

## Slide 4: Core Feature - Level-based Learning (3-4 minutes)

Now let's explore our core learning system.

### The Three-Stage Learning Model

We've developed a scientifically-backed three-stage approach:

**Stage 1: Recognition**  
Students are introduced to new words through interactive flashcards. They see the word, hear the pronunciation, read the definition, and view example sentences. This builds initial familiarity and exposure.

**Stage 2: Spelling**  
Here, we reinforce active recall through dictation and fill-in-the-blank exercises. Students must reproduce the word from memory, strengthening their spelling skills and cementing the word in long-term memory.

**Stage 3: Application**  
The final stage tests contextual understanding. Students complete sentences, choose appropriate words for given contexts, and demonstrate true mastery beyond simple recognition.

### Five Quiz Types

To keep learning fresh and comprehensive, we offer five distinct quiz formats:

1. **Meaning Selection** — Choose the correct definition for a given word
2. **Word Selection** — Select the correct word based on a definition
3. **Spelling Test** — Type out the word from memory
4. **Listening** — Identify words from audio pronunciation
5. **Fill in the Blank** — Complete sentences with appropriate vocabulary

This variety ensures students develop well-rounded vocabulary skills — reading, writing, listening, and contextual application.

## Slide 5: Core Feature - Real-time Battle System (2-3 minutes)

This is where the magic happens — our **Real-time Battle System**.

Students can challenge opponents to vocabulary battles where they race to answer questions correctly. It's not just about accuracy — speed matters too.

### How Battles Work

- Two players are matched based on their grade level
- Both receive the same 10 vocabulary questions
- Each correct answer scores points
- The player with the most points wins
- Winners earn experience points, coins, and climb the rankings

### The Rank Tier System

We've implemented a complete competitive ranking system, similar to popular esports games:

- **Bronze** — Entry level for new players
- **Silver** — Showing consistent improvement
- **Gold** — Solid vocabulary skills
- **Platinum** — Above average performance
- **Diamond** — Elite vocabulary warriors
- **Champion** — The very best, top 1%

This ranking system provides clear goals and creates healthy competition. Students are motivated to practice more to climb the ladder and show off their rank to friends.
